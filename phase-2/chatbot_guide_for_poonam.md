# CHATBOT RESPONSE GUIDELINES

## Big Five Personality → Response Style
- High Neuroticism: Anxious, fearful, seeks reassurance  
- High Agreeableness: Trusting, kind, avoids conflict  
- High Conscientiousness: Careful, rule-following, risk-averse  
- High Extraversion: Social, enthusiastic, seeks attention  
- High Openness: Curious, experimental, tech-friendly  

## Scam Response Mapping (Psychology-Based)

### Romance Scams
**Targets:** High Agreeableness + High Neuroticism  
**Psychology:** Exploits need for emotional connection and anxiety about relationships  
**Estimated Success:** High (based on emotional vulnerability studies)  
**Source:** Cross, C., Richards, K., & Smith, R. (2016). *The reporting experiences and support needs of victims of online fraud*. Journal of Criminology Research.  
https://doi.org/10.1177/2631309X1600200204  
Federal Trade Commission (FTC), 2023. *Romance Scams Cost Consumers Billions*.  
https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/data-visualizations/data-spotlight/2023/02/romance-scams  
**[SIMULATION ONLY]**

### Investment Scams
**Targets:** High Neuroticism + High Conscientiousness  
**Psychology:** Exploits fear of loss and desire for financial security  
**Estimated Success:** Medium–High  
**Source:** Foster, F. D., & Viswanathan, S. (1996). *Strategic Trading When Agents Forecast the Forecasts of Others*. Journal of Finance.  
https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.1996.tb05209.x  
Financial Conduct Authority (FCA), 2022. *Investment fraud and consumer psychology*.  
https://www.fca.org.uk/scamsmart  
**[SIMULATION ONLY]**

### Tech Support Scams
**Targets:** High Openness  
**Psychology:** Exploits curiosity and interest in new technology  
**Estimated Success:** Medium  
**Source:** Modic, D., & Lea, S. (2013). *How neuroticism predicts susceptibility to fraud*. Personality and Individual Differences.  
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.paid.2013.05.002  
FBI IC3, 2023. *Tech Support Fraud Statistics*.  
https://www.ic3.gov/Media/PDF/AnnualReport/2023_IC3Report.pdf

### Social Media / Giveaway Scams
**Targets:** High Extraversion  
**Psychology:** Exploits desire for visibility, excitement, and social rewards  
**Estimated Success:** Medium  
**Source:** Marwick, A., & Boyd, D. (2011). *To See and Be Seen: Celebrity Practice on Twitter*. Convergence Journal.  
https://doi.org/10.1177/1354856510394539  
Krombholz, K., Hobel, H., Huber, M., & Weippl, E. (2015). *Advanced social engineering attacks*. Journal of Information Security.

## Do and Don’t Rules
**Do**
- Maintain consistency with detected personality traits  
- Use psychologically aligned response strategies  

**Don’t**
- Switch response personality mid-conversation  
- Generate or request real personal data
